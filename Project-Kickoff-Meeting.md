### **2 Communication** 
The following communication channels were finalized to engage in team communication and discuss actions:

* Slack: For team collaborative communication and sharing of code snippets and files.
* Whatsapp: For quick real-time communication and error resolutions.
* Google Meet: For scheduled stand-up meetings to discuss progress and blockers.

---

### **3 Methodology**
The following development methodologies and approaches were finalized: 
* Agile: It was finalized that the development methodology would be Agile with time-boxed sprints due to the tight deliverable timelines. 
* MVP (Minimum Viable Product): It was finalized that MVP prototyping design and development strategy would be used to create and early version of the product, thus moving directly into development and building or improving the product further on top of the same, enabling to adhere to deliverable timelines.
* The team will adopt OOAD (Object-Oriented Analysis and Design) techniques as part of the development process. This approach ensures a structured system design and complements the Agile methodology.

---

### **4 Scenario** 
The scenario is a Business Process system for a Wedding Planning Company to manage their internal operations as well as customer relationship operations:

The System consists of the following types of operations:
* User Management: A CRM (Customer Relationship Management) style implementation where wedding planners can manage multiple weddings and customer interactions and updates.
* E-commerce Management: An e-commerce style implementation with a cart mechanism for vendor management and selection of services.
* Project Management: A project management style implementation with a dashboard, with checklists, timeline, and collaborations.

---

### **5 Timeline** 
The project is to be delivered by the 7th April 2025.

The following was decided due to the tight timeline.
* A fully functional MVP prototype is to be completed by 28th March 2025.
* A buffer period of 10 days from 28th March 2025 to 7th April 2025 to resolve errors, system improvements, and documentation.

---

### **6 Tech Stack**
The following was finalized as the technological stack to proceed with the development actions:  
- **As it is a requirement to use MVC(Model-View-Controller) architecture as a design pattern in development.
- **ASP.NET core Razor Pages as Frontend Web Application Framework.
- **C# for Backend Implementation.
- **Entity Framework (EF) Object-relational mapping (ORM) for relational data management.
- **MSSQL Server for database management.

### **7 Work Breakdown Structure**
The following was finalized as a methodology to share respective tasks among team members:

* It is to understand the individual strengths in the overall aspects of development.
* Sharing of tasks in such scenarios where individual strengths can be utilized.
* Members to support one another and knowledge sharing in development aspects if there is an upskill requirement for a member to perform the assigned tasks.
* Each member is responsible for their part in the implementation and supporting other members in troubleshooting and error resolution when required.

---

### **8 Responsibilities**
* Team members are to be assigned and allocated tasks according to their respective strengths in the technologies used in the development, taking into account the tight timeline for development. 
* Frontend capable team members to engage in frontend development.
* Backend capable team members to engage in backend development.
* However, it is important that team members are completely aware of the Frontend and Backend technologies, implementations and entire work structure of the system.
* It is encouraged that team members take upon a complete implementation from frontend to backend to experience the full spectrum of the technology.

### **10 Project Management**
The following tools and approaches were finalized in managing the project:
* Github: Github will be used for version controlling and development collaboration among team members.
* Trello: Trello is a limited free and excellent tool in managing a Project Board.
* Kanban: Kanban approach will enable the smooth flow of a task towards completion through different statuses or columns.
* Task Cards: Can be on Backlog, Design, To-Do, Doing or In-Progress, Code Review, Testing and Done.
* Checklists: Checklists within a single task card can have all the actions related to that component and the team member can check them in the progression of the activity.
*  Project View: It will provide a full view of the standing of the project, work done and pending, deliverables and timeline to achieve.
